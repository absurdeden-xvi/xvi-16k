# Demo Video Script / 演示视频稿

Target length: 2 minutes 30 seconds. The final video must remain under three minutes, include narration, and be public on YouTube.

## English narration and shot list

### 0:00–0:15 — The problem

**Screen:** Open XVI on the blank writing panel. Briefly show the finished long image before returning to the editor.

**Voiceover:**

> XVI is a privacy-first longform typesetting studio for Chinese writers. It turns a finished article into a carefully composed image without uploading the writing, imported fonts, or generated artwork to a server.

### 0:15–0:40 — Write first, compose second

**Screen:** Paste a short Chinese article, add a title and byline, select one sentence, and apply bold or italic styling. Open the script menu and convert the selection to Traditional Chinese.

**Voiceover:**

> The workflow begins with writing, not a constantly shifting preview. I can paste the complete text, preserve inline emphasis, and convert only the selected passage between Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese for Hong Kong, and Traditional Chinese for Taiwan.

### 0:40–1:05 — Automatic composition

**Screen:** Press Compose. Show the generated image, then click the title and body directly in the preview.

**Voiceover:**

> One click creates a readable starting point based on the article length. The title, body size, line height, paragraph spacing, margins, and first-paragraph treatment remain adjustable. The generated canvas is also editable directly, so refinement feels immediate without interrupting the original draft.

### 1:05–1:32 — A visual system, not a color filter

**Screen:** Switch between Folio, Book page, Letter, and Section page. Show two light palettes and one dark palette. Briefly adjust a font, first-line indent, and opening-paragraph treatment.

**Voiceover:**

> XVI includes four editorial structures and a curated color system rather than random background swaps. Templates change hierarchy and reading rhythm, while named palettes, local font import, indentation, alignment, and opening-paragraph controls let each piece develop its own visual character.

### 1:32–1:50 — Export and privacy

**Screen:** Open Export. Show PNG and JPG, the three pixel widths, then export an image. Open the feedback dialog only briefly.

**Voiceover:**

> Export supports PNG and JPG at three explicit resolutions. Canvas rendering rebuilds the document from the same rich-text runs and Chinese line-breaking rules used by the preview. Everything stays local except feedback the user deliberately sends through the separate form.

### 1:50–2:20 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Screen:** Show a short scroll through the GitHub README, changelog, and a few dated commits. Optionally include one still image of the Codex build conversation.

**Voiceover:**

> During OpenAI Build Week, I used Codex with GPT-5.6 as an iterative product and engineering partner. It helped turn detailed visual critique into implementation, debug rich-text parity across the editor, DOM preview, and Canvas export, design regional Chinese conversion, improve mobile interaction, audit privacy boundaries, and maintain bilingual documentation. The key product decisions remained mine: users finish the text before composition, their writing is never uploaded, and templates must change structure rather than simply recolor the same page.

### 2:20–2:32 — Closing

**Screen:** Return to the finished composition, then show the XVI mark and live URL.

**Voiceover:**

> XVI began as a personal tool for writers who care about both language and presentation. It is now a working public preview, and the next step is reliable multi-image slicing and a stronger mobile publishing workflow.

## Recording checklist

- Record at 1440p or 1080p in a desktop browser.
- Keep the browser zoom at 100% and close unrelated tabs.
- Use an article short enough that each action is immediately visible.
- Remove loading time, typing pauses, and repeated adjustments.
- Do not use copyrighted music or third-party visual assets.
- Upload to YouTube as **Public**, not Unlisted or Private.
- Verify the final URL in a signed-out or private browser window.
